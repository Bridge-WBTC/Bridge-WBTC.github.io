What is Bridge WBTC?

Jumper is a multichain routing app for swaps and cross-chain transfers, while WBTC is the wrapped Bitcoin token described in the WBTC network overview. Use the app to select WBTC, compare the route shown, and continue only on the verified domain.

The ERC-20 standard is defined in EIP-20 token methods and events, which is why WBTC approvals, balances, and transfers appear as token-contract actions rather than native BTC transactions.

WBTC bridge supported networks

WBTC started as an Ethereum ERC-20 token and is widely used across EVM DeFi, including Ethereum, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, Base, and other networks through canonical or third-party bridge routes. The important point is that WBTC on one chain is not automatically the same contract as WBTC on another chain. Users should verify the token address, bridge route, and destination app support before transferring. For the Ethereum canonical token, Etherscan's WBTC contract page lists WBTC at 0x2260fac5e5542a773aa44fbcfedf7c193bc2c599.

How to bridge WBTC without selling Bitcoin exposure

Bridging WBTC lets a user move wrapped Bitcoin exposure between chains without swapping into ETH, USDC, or another asset first. The route usually locks, burns, mints, or transfers a wrapped representation depending on the bridge design. Before confirming, compare the quoted received amount, bridge fee, source-chain gas, and destination-chain gas needs; LI.FI's routing documentation describes how routes are calculated across bridges, DEXs, and gas costs in its cross-chain routing overview. WBTC itself represents Bitcoin exposure, but cross-chain bridge risk is separate from WBTC custody risk, so the safest route is one with transparent contracts, enough liquidity, and a token version accepted by your destination protocol.

WBTC bridge fees and slippage

WBTC bridge fees can include source gas, destination gas, a protocol fee, and slippage if the bridge uses liquidity pools. Canonical bridge routes may avoid swap-style slippage but can be slower or more limited in supported chains. Liquidity bridges can be faster, but a large WBTC transfer may move the quoted price or hit available liquidity limits. Always inspect the final received WBTC amount, not just the headline fee, and compare route context with DefiLlama bridge volume data. If the transfer is large, compare two routes and consider whether the destination app accepts that exact WBTC contract.

WBTC on Arbitrum Optimism and Polygon

WBTC is commonly used on Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, and other EVM networks, but each network has its own token contract and bridge history. A wallet may show the same ticker across networks while the underlying address differs; for example, Arbiscan's WBTC token page shows the Arbitrum contract separately from Ethereum. DeFi markets care about the contract, not only the symbol. If you are bridging WBTC to borrow, lend, or LP, check the protocol's supported asset list first. A bridge that delivers a non-preferred WBTC version can force an extra swap or another bridge transaction.

Bridge WBTC FAQ

Is bridged WBTC the same as WBTC on Ethereum?

Not always. Ethereum WBTC has a specific ERC-20 contract, while bridged WBTC on another chain uses that chain's token contract. The value may track the same Bitcoin-backed asset, but wallets and DeFi apps treat each contract separately; CoinGecko's WBTC asset page is useful market context, not a substitute for verifying the destination contract.

Can I bridge WBTC to Bitcoin?

Bridging WBTC directly to native Bitcoin is usually not the same as a normal EVM bridge. To end with BTC on the Bitcoin network, users generally need a redemption, exchange, custodian, or specialized route that converts WBTC exposure back to native BTC; ethereum.org's bridge primer separates bridge transfers from owning native BTC.

Why is my WBTC bridge quote lower than expected?

The quote may include bridge fees, gas, liquidity-provider fees, slippage, or a different output token version. WBTC is high-value per token, so small percentage fees can still be large in dollar terms. Compare the final received amount and check route conditions against DefiLlama's bridge dashboard before deciding whether the route is acceptable.

What contract should I verify for WBTC?

On Ethereum, the widely recognized WBTC token contract is 0x2260fac5e5542a773aa44fbcfedf7c193bc2c599, shown on Etherscan's verified token view. On other chains, the contract will be different, so use the destination chain's explorer, the bridge confirmation screen, and the receiving protocol's token list.

Can WBTC be bridged to Solana?

WBTC can appear on non-EVM ecosystems through specialized bridges or wrapped representations, but that is different from a simple Ethereum-to-L2 transfer. Users should be careful with ticker names on non-EVM chains and confirm the issuer, bridge route, and liquidity; the Binance Academy WBTC guide explains why wrapped BTC depends on chain-specific issuance and custody flows.

Should I bridge WBTC or swap to USDC first?

Bridge WBTC if you want to keep Bitcoin exposure and the destination app supports the received WBTC contract. Swap to USDC first if the destination has deeper stablecoin liquidity, cheaper routing, or no useful WBTC market. The decision is a tradeoff between price exposure, bridge liquidity, fees, and what you plan to do after arrival; ethereum.org's ERC-20 guide explains why token standards and contract variants matter.
