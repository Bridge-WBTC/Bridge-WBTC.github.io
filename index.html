What is Bridge WBTC?

Jumper is a multichain routing app for swaps and cross-chain transfers, while WBTC is the wrapped Bitcoin token described on the WBTC official site. Use the app to select WBTC, compare the route shown, and continue only on the verified domain.

How Bridge WBTC works

  1. Open the verified appUse the official domain, connect the wallet that holds WBTC, and confirm the site shown in your wallet prompt.
  2. Choose the routeSelect WBTC as the asset, set the source network, then pick a destination network available in the app.
  3. Review the quoteCheck the bridge provider, estimated output, gas needs, approval request, and recipient address before signing.
  4. Sign and trackApprove only the required token spend, submit the transfer, and follow status in the app or on the relevant explorer.

Bridge WBTC FAQ

What does bridge wbtc mean?

It means moving Wrapped Bitcoin from one supported blockchain network to another through a bridge or routing app while keeping the asset selected as WBTC where available.

Do I need native BTC for this flow?

No. If you already hold WBTC, the app uses WBTC as the asset; you still need the source chain's native gas token and may need destination gas after arrival.

Can I send WBTC to any chain?

No. Use only the source and destination networks shown as available in the official app, because token variants and bridge support can differ by chain.

Why is an approval shown before the transfer?

WBTC is an ERC-20 token, so your wallet may ask you to approve a specific spend before the bridge or router can move the amount you choose.

How do I verify the destination?

Check the app domain, selected networks, recipient address, token symbol, contract details, and transaction status on the relevant explorer before and after signing.
